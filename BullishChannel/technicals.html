Popular Technical Indicators

EMA

EMA (short for Exponentially Moving Average) is a moving average indicator that put more weights on the most recent candles. This in short means that the EMA are more responsive to recent changes in price actions than compared to SMA (Simple Moving Average). A practical use of the EMA is the popular trios of 8-9 EMA, 13EMA, and 20EMA. These short-period EMAs are to act as support or resistance zone as we expect price to bounce off of these points.

SMA

SMA (short for Simple Moving Average) is a moving average indicator that when calculated equally weighs each of the (closing) candles– averaging them to form an average line. A practical use of the SMA is the 50-SMA on the daily chart to show the bullishness (if price is above the 50-SMA) or bearishness (if price is below the 50-SMA) of the current trend of a stock.

VWAP

VWAP (short for Volume Weighted Average Price) is a trend indicator. This unique indicators combines both the price action and volume towards calculation. VWAP is popularly used in intrday-trading in determining its trend (price above VWAP indicates bullishness and vice-versa). VWAP also acts like a magnet– price generally attracts back to the VWAP level.

MACD

Trend-following momentum indicator. MACD line is calculated by subtracting the 26-period EMA (Exponential Moving Average) from the 12-EMA. The signal line is calculated by taking the 9-EMA. The intersection of the converging of these two lines can generate buy (MACD line crosses the Signal line from below) or sell signals (MACD line crosses the Signal line from above).

Momentum

Monument indicator measures the speed of price fluctuations. To derive the calculation, take the latest price subtracted by the closing price of x number of days ago. This indicator can be used to show strengths or weaknesses within a stock to predict the most probable further price actions.

RSI

A momentum indicator. RSI is an oscillator with 2 extreme points. The extreme of 30 indicates areas of oversold level. This oversold level is popularly represented as the area of undervalued price range. The opposite extreme of 70 indicates areas of overbought level. This overbought level is popularly represented as the area of overvalued price range.

Vortex

Trend indicator. The vortex indicator is represented by 2 lines– VI+ and VI-. When the VI+ is above the VI- line, the trend is said to be bullish, while vice-versa. The crossing of these lines represent the change in the current trend.
